# The Journey

A reverse-chronological tour of how a curious kid in Mumbai ended up running an *AI consulting practice* from Boston. Most recent up top — scroll down to walk it backwards.

---
date: 2026-05
tag: today-ish
title: Kathalyst hits its stride
---

Year two of the company. Three enterprise legacy modernization engagements running in parallel, a small team of contractors I trust, and a steady drumbeat of inbound leads. First time building feels less like sprinting and more like compounding.

---
date: 2026-02
tag: hired
title: First full-time hire
---

Brought on a senior modernization engineer. Writing my first offer letter was strangely terrifying — suddenly someone else's mortgage depended on me selling work. The "founder" label finally felt earned.

---
date: 2025-10
tag: shipped
title: First six-figure contract signed
---

A regional bank wanted their COBOL claims system mapped, documented, and partially modernized. I almost talked myself out of the price. Sent the SOW anyway. They signed in 48 hours. Lesson: **charge for the value, not the hours.**

---
date: 2025-06
tag: pivoted
title: Narrowed the niche to legacy + AI
---

Spent six months saying yes to every consulting gig that came in. Realized the work I was best at — and that nobody else seemed to want to touch — was using AI to make legacy code legible. Rewrote the website that night.

---
date: 2025-03
tag: founded
title: Kathalyst, LLC officially incorporated
---

Filed the paperwork on a Tuesday, made a logo on a Wednesday, took my first client call on a Thursday. The name is a portmanteau of *katha* (story) and *catalyst*. It still makes me smile.

---
date: 2025-01
tag: quit
title: Left my full-time job
---

Walked away from a comfortable senior engineer role with three months of runway and one verbal handshake of consulting work. Everyone said I was brave. I was mostly terrified. *The fear is part of the data — not a reason to ignore it.*

---
date: 2024-09
tag: side-hustle
title: First paid consulting gig (nights & weekends)
---

A friend's startup needed someone to audit their AI architecture. Charged half what I should have, delivered double what I promised, learned what scoping actually means. The check cleared. Something shifted.

---
date: 2024-05
tag: learned
title: Started writing in public
---

Began publishing essays on legacy systems and AI — not because I was an expert, but because writing is how I figure out what I think. Three readers turned into thirty turned into the inbound pipeline that made everything else possible.

---
date: 2023-11
tag: experimented
title: First COBOL-meets-LLM prototype
---

Pointed an LLM at a 40-year-old COBOL file at 2am out of pure curiosity. It produced a halfway-decent flowchart. I stared at the screen and realized this was a wedge. *The whole company started here.*

---
date: 2023-03
tag: shipped & sunset
title: Failed indie SaaS attempt
---

Spent six months building a productivity tool nobody asked for. Launched to crickets. Took it down after three paying users churned. The technical lessons were free; the ego lessons were expensive but worth it.

---
date: 2022-08
tag: promoted
title: Senior engineer, mainframe modernization team
---

Joined a team migrating a Fortune 500 from COBOL to a cloud-native stack. Two years of staring into the abyss of forty-year-old business logic. It was supposed to be a job. **It became a thesis.**

---
date: 2021-07
tag: taught
title: Started teaching Kathak workshops on the side
---

My first taste of running something where I was the product. Pricing my own time, marketing to strangers, handling refunds. Tiny scale, huge education. Every founder skill I use today, I rehearsed in a dance studio first.

---
date: 2020-04
tag: shipped
title: First "real" dev job
---

Joined a fintech startup as a junior engineer in the middle of a global lockdown. Learned the shape of a product team, what shipping at velocity feels like, and that imposter syndrome doesn't get cured — just outgrown.

---
date: 2019-12
tag: graduated
title: Engineering degree, with a side of stage
---

Finished my CS degree while performing in three classical dance productions. Learned that running a production schedule and shipping a software project are the same skill in different costumes.

---
date: 2017-06
tag: first-paid
title: Built my first website for money
---

A family friend ran a small bakery and needed a site. I charged the equivalent of forty dollars and stretched it over six weekends. I was nineteen. I haven't stopped since.

---
date: 2008-01
tag: origin story
title: A ten-year-old, a borrowed laptop, and an HTML book
---

I built my first webpage in a Mumbai apartment, mostly to make my parents stop asking what I was doing on the computer. It was hideous. It was mine. *Some part of me has been chasing that feeling ever since.*
